Biography

Dr. Anupom Das is a physician–researcher with experience across clinical medicine, epidemiology, disease surveillance, and health systems research. His work spans frontline clinical care, including management of high-risk patients during the COVID-19 pandemic, as well as roles in government health services, public health emergency response, and clinical research implementation.

He has worked across hospital settings, national surveillance systems, and community-based research, contributing to both patient care and data-informed public health decision-making. This combined clinical and population health experience provides a strong foundation for research at the intersection of health informatics and epidemiology.

He is currently pursuing a Master’s in Health Informatics at Karolinska Institutet and Stockholm University as a recipient of the Swedish Institute Scholarship for Global Professionals (SISGP, 2025–2027).

His research interests include digital health systems, real-world health data, health equity in digital healthcare access, implementation research for clinical workflow improvement, and strengthening disease surveillance systems.

He is actively seeking opportunities to pursue doctoral research and contribute to clinically grounded, data-driven, and policy-relevant health research.
